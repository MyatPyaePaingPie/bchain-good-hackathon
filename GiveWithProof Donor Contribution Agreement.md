# **Donor Contribution Agreement with Grant Advisory Terms**

THIS AGREEMENT is made on this \[Day\] day of \[Month\], \[Year\] (the "Effective Date") by and between \[Name of U.S. 501(c)(3) Partner\] , a tax-exempt public charity organized under the laws of the State of \[State\] with a principal place of business at \[Address\] (the "Sponsor"), and \[Donor Name\] , with an address at \[Address\] (the "Donor").

**RECITALS**

WHEREAS, Sponsor is a public charity described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), and a "sponsoring organization" as defined in Section 4966(d)(1) of the Code, operating a donor-advised fund program;

WHEREAS, Donor wishes to establish a donor-advised fund (the "Fund") with Sponsor to support charitable purposes;

WHEREAS, Donor utilizes the technology platform provided by GiveWithProof (the "Platform") to facilitate transparency, impact reporting, and compliance tooling, but the Platform does not take custody of assets or exercise discretion over grants; and

WHEREAS, Donor acknowledges that all contributions made to Sponsor are irrevocable and that Sponsor retains final discretion and control over all grants distributed from the Fund.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

**1\. DEFINITIONS**

1.1 “Program” means a specific charitable initiative, project, or funding area described in detail on the Platform or in Sponsor’s supporting documentation. Each Program shall have:

1) a defined charitable purpose;  
2) a description of the activities to be carried out;  
3) the intended beneficiary population or geographic region; and  
4) a projected budget or funding goal.

1.2 “Selected Program” means a Program that Donor affirmatively chooses when making a contribution or submitting a grant recommendation. Selection of a Program constitutes a non-binding advisory preference, not a legal restriction.

1.3 “Negative Preference” means a Program that Donor explicitly indicates (via the Platform or in writing) as a Program to which Donor does not wish their contribution to be applied. Sponsor shall respect Negative Preferences to the fullest extent permissible by law, provided that doing so does not impair Sponsor’s exclusive legal control or ability to carry out its charitable purposes.

1.4 “Surplus” means the situation where, at the time of allocation, the total funds designated (by all donors) for a particular Program exceed the amount that Sponsor determines can be reasonably and effectively deployed for the Program’s charitable activities within a reasonable period, taking into account the Program’s budget, capacity, and any applicable legal or operational constraints.

### **2\. Establishment of Fund and Irrevocability**

2.1 Establishment. Donor hereby establishes a donor-advised fund with Sponsor. The initial contribution is set forth in Exhibit A attached hereto.

2.2 Irrevocability Clause. All contributions made by Donor to the Fund are irrevocable. Donor acknowledges that once a contribution is made to Sponsor, the Donor retains no legal or equitable right, title, or interest in the contributed assets. Donor shall not have the power to revoke, modify, or request the return of any contribution or the income earned thereon. All contributions are subject to the exclusive legal control of Sponsor.

### **3\. Discretion and Control**

3.1 No Donor Control Clause. Donor acknowledges and agrees that Sponsor is not merely a conduit for Donor’s charitable intent. Sponsor exercises exclusive legal control over the Fund. Donor understands that the Fund constitutes an asset of Sponsor and that Sponsor has the ultimate authority and discretion to accept or reject any grant recommendation.

3.2 Discretion Clause (Critical for DAF Compliance). In accordance with Section 4966(d)(2)(A) of the Code, Sponsor has "exclusive legal control" over the assets of the Fund. Final discretion regarding the distribution of any and all grants from the Fund lies solely with the Board of Directors of Sponsor (or its duly authorized designee). The Donor’s role is limited to making non-binding recommendations.

3.3 Non-Binding Recommendations. Donor may submit grant recommendations to Sponsor via the Platform. Such recommendations are advisory only. Sponsor may reject any recommendation for any reason, including but not limited to:

1) Failure to satisfy Equivalency Determination or Expenditure Responsibility requirements for international organizations;  
2) The proposed grantee not being a "public charity" under Section 509(a)(1), (2), or (3) of the Code;  
3) Non-compliance with the Sanctions Programs administered by the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC);  
4) Violations of state fundraising laws (e.g., California Supervision of Trustees and Fundraisers for Charitable Purposes Act); or  
5) The recommendation violates the prohibition against impermissible private benefit or inurement.

### **4\. International Grantmaking and Tax Compliance**

4.1 Foreign Grantees. Donor acknowledges that if Donor recommends a grant to an organization that is not recognized by the IRS as a tax-exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) (a "Foreign Entity"), Sponsor must comply with strict IRS regulations to ensure the grant is a qualifying distribution.  
4.2 Compliance Documentation. For any grant to a Foreign Entity, Sponsor shall require either: 

1) A valid Equivalency Determination (ED) confirming the Foreign Entity is the equivalent of a U.S. public charity; or  
2) Expenditure Responsibility (ER) procedures, requiring detailed reporting and oversight.

4.3 Form 1023 Clarification. Donor acknowledges that a U.S. Form 1023 is not applicable to foreign entities. The Donor agrees to facilitate, or cause the Foreign Entity to facilitate, the submission of all required documentation (including detailed budgets, audited financials, and registration statuses) to Sponsor to satisfy ED or ER requirements. Failure to provide such documentation shall result in the denial of the grant recommendation.

### **5\. No Earmarking and Allocation of Funds**

5.1 No Earmarking Clause. Donor acknowledges that the Platform is a technology tool for reporting, not a mechanism for earmarking. Donor shall not direct that specific funds be used for a specific, pre-identified individual or discrete purchase unless such use constitutes a direct charitable expenditure permissible under Section 170(c).

5.2 Application to Selected Programs. When Donor makes a contribution to the Fund and selects one or more Programs in order of preference (e.g., first choice, second choice, third choice), Sponsor shall apply the contribution to those Programs in accordance with this Section.

5.3 Project Selection vs. Distribution. Donor retains the ability to select a Project Category (e.g., "Clean Water Initiative," "Educational Scholarship Fund") via the Platform. However, Donor agrees that the selection of a Project Category constitutes a non-binding suggestion of a charitable purpose, not a legal restriction on the use of funds.

5.4 Allocation to First Preference. Sponsor shall first apply the contribution to the Donor’s highest‑priority Selected Program (the “First Preference”), unless doing so would cause or contribute to a Surplus in that Program.

5.5 Handling of Surplus. If, at the time of allocation, Sponsor determines in its sole discretion that the Donor’s First Preference Program has a Surplus or is otherwise unable to accept additional funds for operational or compliance reasons, Sponsor shall instead apply the contribution to the Donor’s next‑highest‑priority Selected Program (Second Preference, then Third Preference, etc.) that does not have a Surplus and is able to accept the contribution.

5.6 Itemized Reporting. Following the distribution of funds, Sponsor shall provide (or the Platform shall display) an Itemized Impact Allocation Report. This report is a good-faith accounting of how funds were allocated across specific activities. Donor acknowledges that this report is an estimate based on aggregate accounting and does not constitute a representation that the Donor’s specific dollar bill was physically transferred to a specific end beneficiary.

5.7 Negative Preference (Exclusion) Guidance. Donor may indicate preferences regarding categories of activities, expenditures, or sub-projects to which the Donor would prefer that their contribution not be allocated. Donor acknowledges and agrees that:

1) Any such Negative Preferences are advisory only and non-binding;  
2) The Sponsor retains full and exclusive discretion and control over the ultimate allocation of funds, notwithstanding any Negative Preferences expressed by the Donor;  
3) Negative Preferences shall be interpreted as general guidance at the Project Category level, and not as legally enforceable restrictions on specific grants, expenditures, or recipients; and  
4) The Sponsor may, in its sole discretion, disregard or modify any Negative Preferences to ensure compliance with applicable law, operational feasibility, or charitable objectives.

Donor further acknowledges that the inclusion of Negative Preferences does not create any right to direct, restrict, or condition the use of contributed funds and does not alter the irrevocable nature of the contribution.

### **6\. Limitations of Liability and Disclaimers**

6.1 Limitation of Liability. TO THE FULLEST EXTENT PERMITTED BY LAW, SPONSOR SHALL NOT BE LIABLE TO DONOR OR ANY THIRD PARTY FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THE MANAGEMENT OF THE FUND, THE ACCEPTANCE OR REJECTION OF GRANT RECOMMENDATIONS, OR THE USE OF THE PLATFORM, EVEN IF SPONSOR HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES. SPONSOR’S TOTAL LIABILITY TO DONOR FOR ANY CLAIM ARISING OUT OF THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE AMOUNT OF THE DONOR’S MOST RECENT CONTRIBUTION TO THE FUND.

6.2 Data Accuracy Disclaimer. THE PLATFORM PROVIDES TRANSPARENCY DASHBOARDS, IMPACT REPORTING, AND ITEMIZED RECEIPTS FOR INFORMATIONAL PURPOSES ONLY. WHILE SPONSOR AND THE PLATFORM USE COMMERCIALLY REASONABLE EFFORTS TO ENSURE ACCURACY, SPONSOR DOES NOT WARRANT THAT THE IMPACT REPORTS OR ALLOCATION DATA ARE ERROR-FREE, COMPLETE, OR AUDIT-QUALITY. THE REPRESENTATION OF "TRACING" IS CONCEPTUAL (E.G., "YOUR DONATION CONTRIBUTED TO…") AND NOT A LITERAL ACCOUNTING OF THE PATH OF SPECIFIC CURRENCY.

### **7\. Fees and Platform Use**

7.1 Administrative Fees. Sponsor reserves the right to deduct reasonable administrative fees from the Fund to cover the costs of grant administration, compliance (including Equivalency Determination and Expenditure Responsibility), and reporting.

7.2 Technology Fees. Donor acknowledges that the Platform is operated by GiveWithProof, a separate for-profit entity. Any fees charged by the Platform (e.g., SaaS fees, platform fees) are separate from the charitable contribution and shall be paid directly to the Platform. Sponsor does not receive economic benefit from such fees except as disclosed in compliance with Revenue Ruling 2006-41.

### **8\. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution**

8.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of \[State\], without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

8.2 Arbitration. Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved through binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association (AAA) in \[City, State\], except that either party may seek injunctive relief in a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent the misappropriation of charitable assets.

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements, whether written or oral.
